### Modeling Capabilities

This document summarizes all modeling components in Owl (Optimal wealth lab), how each is implemented, and the assumptions and limitations that apply. It serves as a quick reference for understanding the scope and boundaries of the optimizer.

| Component | Modeling approach | Assumptions & limitations |
|------------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **Objectives** | maxSpending: maximize net spending subject to bequest floor. maxBequest: maximize after-tax bequest subject to spending floor; requires `netSpending` option to set the spending floor. Roth conversion amounts optimized in both (subject to caps and per-person controls). Optional `timePreference` (%/year) discounts future spending to reduce end-of-life back-loading. Optional `fixedSpending` (maxSpending only) pins first-year spending to a fixed dollar value; profile slack then varies later years dynamically rather than optimizing the basis. | LP objective is linear, so it always drives spending to an extreme of the feasible range; use profile slack and bequest/spending constraints to shape the solution. |
| **Spending profile** | User-specified shape (flat or smile) scaling a first-year basis. Survivorship factor at first death. Optimizer determines achievable basis. The profile is a bilateral constraint for both objectives: spending stays within ±slack% of the profile shape (`spendingSlack` sets the tolerance). | Profile shape fixed; only scale is optimized. |
| **Bequest objective** | After-tax estate value. Tax-deferred and HSA balances discounted by heirs' marginal rate. Surviving spouse inherits per beneficiary fractions; non-spouse portion taxed. | Single heirs' marginal tax rate applied to all inherited tax-deferred and HSA balances. |
| **Taxable accounts** | Dividend and interest yield proportional to balance each year; capital gains when optimizer elects to sell. All realized gains treated as long-term. Capital gains on withdrawals use the **average-cost** method: when a fraction of the account is sold, the same fraction of the total cost basis is recovered, so the realized gain equals `gain_fraction × withdrawal` where `gain_fraction = (balance − basis) / balance`. Optional: `setCostBasis([b1, b2])` (or TOML field `taxable_cost_basis`) supplies the current cost basis per person; the SC loop then tracks how basis evolves each year as shares are sold and new contributions (HFP contributions plus LP surplus deposits) add fresh basis. Without a supplied basis, realized gains fall back to current-year price appreciation only (`(τ₀ − μ) × withdrawal`), which underestimates gains for accounts with substantial embedded appreciation. | All gains treated as long-term; short-term gains (holding period &lt; 1 year) not distinguished. Average-cost method only; FIFO and LIFO lot accounting not supported. Tax-loss harvesting not modeled. |
| **Safety net (taxable)** | Optional inflation-indexed minimum balance per individual's taxable account. Enforced from year 2 through each individual's life horizon. | First year excluded. Can cause infeasibility if minimum exceeds initial balance or desired bequest is smaller than survivor's safety net. |
| **Tax-deferred accounts** (401k, IRA, 403b) | Pre-tax contributions from Wages and Contributions table. Withdrawals taxed as ordinary income. RMDs enforced as minimum withdrawal floors from age 70–75 (birth-year dependent). 10% early withdrawal penalty before age 59½. | Pro-rata rule (IRS Form 8606) not modeled; entire balance treated as pre-tax. Early withdrawal exceptions (72(t), disability, etc.) not modeled. |
| **Roth accounts** | After-tax contributions. Five-year maturation rule enforced via minimum-balance constraints (recent conversions + contribution gains retained). Age 59½ threshold per individual. Conversion amounts optimized; taxable as ordinary income in year of conversion. | Contribution principal not separately tracked; some valid early withdrawals of principal conservatively disallowed. Pro-rata rule not modeled. Roth conversions disallowed in last two years of plan horizon. |
| **Health Savings Accounts (HSA)** | Fourth account type. Pre-tax contributions reduce ordinary income, provisional income, and MAGI. Contributions zeroed at Medicare enrollment age (IRC §223: Medicare disqualifies contributions). Withdrawals capped at total qualified medical expenses (QMEs): Medicare costs plus `setMedicalExpenses()` amount (dental, vision, co-pays, etc.). Pre-Medicare years: only the `setMedicalExpenses()` amount is eligible. Spouse inherits intact. | IRS contribution limits not enforced; user responsible. HDHP eligibility assumed, not verified. Catch-up (\$1,000 for 55+) not auto-applied. Mid-year Medicare: entire year zeroed (IRS allows proration). Pre-65 non-qualified penalty (20%) not modeled for withdrawals exceeding QMEs. |
| **Fixed assets** | Real estate, annuities, collectibles, etc. Disposition generates proceeds split into ordinary income, LTCG, and tax-free portions by asset type. Growth rate from acquisition to disposition year. Sales commission applied. **Type-specific tax treatment:** `residence` — IRC §121 primary residence exclusion applied ($250k single / $500k MFJ); gain above the exclusion is LTCG, excluded gain and basis are tax-free. `fixed annuity` — gain above basis is ordinary income; basis returned tax-free. All other types (`real estate`, `stocks`, `collectibles`, `precious metals`) — full gain is LTCG, basis is tax-free. | Fixed outflows; disposition timing and amount user-specified. No optimization of sale timing. |
| **Debts** | Amortized payments from start year for term years. Principal and interest from standard amortization. Enters cash-flow as fixed outflows. | No optimization of paydown vs. invest. Mortgage interest not deducted (standard deduction assumed). |
| **Wages & contributions** | Year-indexed table: wages, other income, net inv, contributions to all account types, Roth conversions, big-ticket items. Five-year lookback for Roth maturation. | User-supplied; no optimization of contribution amounts. HSA contributions zeroed at Medicare age. |
| **Social Security benefits** | PIA × age-based adjustment factor. FRA by birth year (65–67). Own, spousal, and survivor benefits computed from PIA and DOB. First-year proration by fraction of year remaining (same as pension). Taxability (0–85%) from provisional income formula converged via the SC loop. Optional trim for trust-fund scenarios. Claiming ages are user-specified by default. Optional: `withSSTaxability="optimize"` replaces the SC-loop taxability approximation with an exact MILP formulation. Optional: `withSSAges="optimize"` co-optimizes SS claiming ages (monthly resolution, 62–70) via MILP. | Family maximum benefit cap not modeled. Earnings test not modeled; users assumed fully retired before FRA. Survivor benefits assumed claimed in year of spouse's death; deferral strategy not optimized. WEP and GPO repealed (2025); not modeled. |
| **Pension income** | Monthly amount and commencement age per individual. Optional inflation indexing. First-year proration by fraction of year remaining. Joint-and-survivor fraction for survivor benefit. | Early-commencement reductions and late-commencement credits not modeled. |
| **SPIA (Single Premium Immediate Annuity)** | IRA/401k rollover funds a SPIA: premium deducted from tax-deferred account in buy year (non-taxable event); annual payments begin in the same year as purchase as fully taxable ordinary income. Optional CPI indexing. Optional joint-and-survivor fraction for couples. Payments increase MAGI, affecting Medicare IRMAA surcharges and Social Security taxability. Multiple SPIAs supported per plan. | Premium assumed drawn entirely from tax-deferred account (IRA rollover); non-IRA funding not modeled. Partial-year proration of income in buy year not modeled (full annual payment assumed). Annuity pricing (premium-to-income ratio) is user-supplied; no built-in pricing model. |
| **Ordinary income tax** | Seven brackets (OBBBA 2026: 10–37%). Standard deduction + 65+ additional + OBBBA §1002 bonus (phases out by MAGI). Bracket widths and rates inflation-adjusted. Optional reversion to pre-TCJA rates. | Itemized deductions not modeled; standard deduction only. OBBBA 65+ bonus phaseout computed outside LP (post-solution update). |
| **State income tax** | All 50 states + DC supported via `state` field in `[basic_info]`. Graduated brackets with per-year inflation adjustment. Filing status transitions MFJ→Single at spouse death. State standard deduction. Optional retirement income exemption (age-gated dollar cap, e.g. NY \$20k, CO \$24k age 65+). Optional pension exemption (e.g. KY \$31,110, IL fully exempt). Optional SS exclusion (e.g. FL/TX zero SS tax; KS SS exempt below \$75k AGI — binary flag, not phased out). Capital gains taxed as ordinary income at state level. All state LP variables are continuous (pure LP, no new binary variables). | State AMT, local taxes, state-specific credits not modeled. SS threshold exemption (KS \$75k, MO \$100k) treated as binary (always taxed) — conservative for high-income plans. Retirement income exemption applies to IRA withdrawals only; SPIA and annuity income treated as ordinary. Bracket rates in `taxes_state.toml` require annual review. |
| **Long-term capital gains tax** | Three brackets (0%, 15%, 20%) based on total taxable income. LTCG stacks on ordinary income. By default, bracket thresholds are determined via the SC loop (prior-year ordinary income fixes bracket bounds; optimizer fills lowest-rate brackets first). Optional: `withLTCG="optimize"` replaces the SC-loop approximation with an exact MILP formulation using binary variables (`zl`) to select the correct 0%/15%/20% bracket each year. | All realized gains treated as long-term; short-term gains not modeled. |
| **Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)** | 3.8% on lesser of NII and MAGI excess over \$200k (single) / \$250k (MFJ). NII = dividends, interest, LTCG, rent/trust from `net inv` column. Converged via self-consistent loop. Optional: `withNIIT="optimize"` uses exact MILP formulation with binary variables (`zj`) to determine whether MAGI exceeds the threshold each year; most effective when `withLTCG="optimize"` is also set. | Thresholds not indexed for inflation (per IRS). |
| **Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)** | IRS Uniform Lifetime Table III for standard cases; IRS Joint and Last Survivor Table II (Pub 590-B) when spouse is the sole beneficiary and more than 10 years younger. Starting age: 70 (pre-1949), 72 (1949–1950), 73 (1951–1959), 75 (1960+). Fractions applied to prior-year-end balance. | Inherited IRA and beneficiary RMD rules not modeled. |
| **Medicare Part B & Part D** | Part B and Part D premiums (base + IRMAA surcharges) from MAGI two years prior. Part D uses same brackets as Part B; optional base premium (default 0) and optional disable for other drug coverage. By default, IRMAA surcharges are determined via the SC loop (prior MAGI fixes bracket selection each iteration). Optional: `withMedicare="optimize"` replaces the SC-loop approximation with an exact MILP formulation for bracket selection; can be disabled to reduce MIP size and improve solve time when IRMAA precision is not needed. | IRMAA thresholds assumed to scale with general inflation; CMS may use different index. First two plan years require user-supplied prior MAGI (default zero). Part D base is user-configurable; default 0. |
| **ACA marketplace (pre-65)** | Net Premium Tax Credit (PTC) cost: user sets SLCSP benchmark premium; PTC computed from MAGI and FPL. For couples, the SLCSP should be the combined household premium; when the older spouse transitions to Medicare, the SLCSP is automatically scaled to the remaining spouse's individual plan using the CMS age rating curve (45 CFR 147.102), prorating by age-rating factors at the transition year. Optional start year defers ACA coverage (years before start treated as employer-covered, zero ACA cost). Two modes: **loop** (default, `withACA` unset or `"loop"`) uses SC iteration with year-aware acaCosts; **optimize** (`withACA="optimize"`) uses LP/MIP to co-optimize MAGI vs ACA cost. Below 138% FPL returns full SLCSP (loop) or 2.1% (optimize; Medicaid approximation). | **Loop**: Year-aware (2025/2026 rules). Exact piecewise linear. ACA fixed per MAGI each iteration. **Optimize**: 2026 rules only; no year-awareness. Constant-per-bracket approximation. LP can reduce ACA by shifting MAGI. Above 400% FPL: full SLCSP (both modes). SLCSP user-supplied; FPL updated annually. Start year applies to both modes. Couple SLCSP scaling assumes user supplied the combined household premium; individual-only plans should use half the joint premium. |
| **Cash-flow balance** | Annual equality: spending + taxes + Medicare + debt payments + contributions = wages + SS + pension + withdrawals + fixed-asset proceeds + other income. All amounts inflation-adjusted (today's dollars). Big-ticket items shift spending into specific years. | Single annual time step; no intra-year cash-flow detail. |
| **Asset allocation** | User-specified glide path (linear or s-curve) from initial to final allocation across four asset classes (S&P 500, corporate bonds, T-notes, cash). Per-account or per-individual or household-wide. | Accounts assumed rebalanced to target allocation. No optimization of allocation itself. |
| **Return rate models** | Constant: trailing_30, user, conservative, optimistic, historical_average. Varying: historical (replay), historical_gaussian, historical_lognormal, gaussian, lognormal, historical_bootstrap, vector_ar, garch_dcc, historical_copula (Gaussian copula — fits a 4×4 copula correlation matrix to the historical window; each asset's marginal distribution is preserved exactly via the empirical CDF with no parametric shape imposed; generates new year-combinations that honour all pairwise rank correlations), gmm (Gaussian Mixture Model — fits K multivariate Gaussian components on the historical window to capture regime-dependent cross-asset correlations; independent draws), hmm (Hidden Markov Model — extends GMM with a Markov transition matrix between K regimes fitted via Baum-Welch EM; adds temporal autocorrelation and regime persistence across years). Custom DataFrame model or pluggable Python modules via API. Optional reverse (time-reversal) and roll sequence transforms for varying methods. Optional `constrain_mean` flag (histogaussian, histolognormal, historical_copula, garch_dcc, gmm, hmm) shifts each generated series additively so its arithmetic mean matches the historical window mean, eliminating sampling bias without altering distribution shape. | Constant rates: no sequence-of-returns risk. Historical: plan horizon must fit in selected window. Stochastic methods require sufficient history for fitting. For parametric Gaussian-residual models (historical_gaussian, historical_lognormal, vector_ar, garch_dcc), a piecewise-linear (PWL) normalization transform is automatically applied to the inflation dimension before fitting to correct for the right-skewness of historical inflation; slopes are auto-calibrated from the selected date window and reported in the debug log. |
| **Simulation modes** | Historical back-test: replays past return sequences from selected window. Monte Carlo: stochastic sampling (historical_bootstrap, historical_gaussian, lognormal, etc.). Fixed-rate: constant or user-supplied returns. Stochastic spending optimization: solves a recourse LP across S scenarios to find a committed first-year spending level $g^*$ that maximizes spending subject to a target shortfall probability; sweeps risk-aversion parameter $\lambda$ to trace the efficient frontier (spending vs. expected shortfall). Optional longevity risk (MC-only) samples ages-at-death from selected mortality table by sex (`M`/`F`) and current age; each scenario is solved on its own sampled horizon (last-survivor for couples). | Historical: plan horizon must fit in data window; single deterministic path. Monte Carlo: sequence-of-returns risk; convergence depends on sample size. Historical-range augmented sampling uses reverse × roll combinations. Stochastic spending LP: non-anticipativity enforced only on first-year commitment; later years adapt to each scenario. Longevity+MC reproducibility requires separate seeding of both rate and longevity RNG streams. |
| **Retirement Efficiency Score (RES)** | Experimental Sharpe-ratio analog for historical scenarios: RES = (committed spending − floor) / CVaR, where CVaR is the floor-capped conditional value-at-risk of the shortfall distribution. The floor defaults to the HSF (historical spending floor). RES is maximized over the efficient frontier to find the optimal target success rate ρ\*. Available via `compute_res()` and `compute_cvar()` in the public API; shown as an experimental expander in the Spending Optimization UI (historical mode only). | Experimental; not validated for production use. MC RES is not exposed in the UI because the lognormal tail structure pushes ρ\* to artificially aggressive values (~30%) that do not reflect practical spending risk. |
| **Filing status** | MFJ until first spouse's death; then single. Standard deduction, brackets, SS thresholds, LTCG thresholds, and Medicare premiums transition automatically. | Head of household not modeled. Death year fixed at plan construction for the deterministic LP solve; stochastic mortality (sampled lifespans by sex and mortality table) is supported for Monte Carlo longevity-risk analysis (see Simulation modes). |
| **Self-consistent (SC) loop** | Iteratively fixes nonlinear quantities (SS taxability, NIIT, LTCG bracket bounds, Medicare IRMAA, OBBBA phaseout) at previous values, solves LP, recomputes, repeats until convergence. | Convergence not guaranteed in edge cases. Optional MIP modes (`withSSTaxability="optimize"`, `withLTCG="optimize"`, `withNIIT="optimize"`, `withACA="optimize"`, `withMedicare="optimize"`) for exact treatment at higher computational cost. |
| **MIP decomposition** | When multiple `"optimize"` flags are active simultaneously, the monolithic MILP may be slow (~400 binary variables). Two decomposition strategies are available via `withDecomposition`. **Sequential** (relax-and-fix): solves LP relaxation, rounds all bracket binary families (`zm`, `za`, `zs`, `zl`, `zj`) simultaneously, then solves a single MIP with brackets fixed and `zx` free; fast heuristic. **Benders**: classical Benders decomposition separating bracket selection (master MIP: `zm`, `za`, `zs`, `zl`, `zj`) from continuous planning (subproblem LP/MIP: continuous variables + `zx`); generates dual-based optimality cuts to certify global optimality within the specified MIP gap. Both modes fall back to monolithic MIP when no bracket binaries are present. Both HiGHS and MOSEK supported. | Sequential mode is a heuristic (not globally optimal). Benders guarantees global optimality but requires solving 2–3 problems per iteration. Convergence typically occurs in 1–3 Benders iterations in practice. |
| **Beneficiary fractions** | Fraction of each account type left to surviving spouse at first death. Default 1.0 for all (including HSA). When not all 1.0, triggers binary variables for exact modeling. | Can significantly increase solve time when fractions differ across accounts. |

### Access interfaces

Owl's modeling capabilities are available through four interfaces:

| Interface | How to use |
|-----------|------------|
| **Streamlit web UI** | Browser-based interactive tool — no installation. Hosted at [owlplanner.streamlit.app](https://owlplanner.streamlit.app) or self-hosted. |
| **Python API / Jupyter** | Import `owlplanner` directly; build and solve `Plan` objects programmatically. See `USER_GUIDE.md`. |
| **CLI (`owlcli`)** | Run, compare, and explain cases from the terminal using TOML case files. |
| **AI assistant (MCP)** | Connect Owl as a tool to Claude, Cursor, Zed, VS Code (Copilot or Cline), or any MCP-compatible AI client. Describe your financial situation in plain language; the AI runs the optimizer and interprets the results. See `docs/mcp.md`. |
